My academic journey began with a major in Software Engineering. After several years working as a programmer, I pursued my passion for language studies. I returned to academia, earning a BA in English Translation from Qazvin University and an MA in Linguistics from Allameh Tabataba'i University (Tehran).

The integration of my expertise in both fields led to my collaboration on interdisciplinary work in computational linguistics at Ruhr University Bochum focusing on Biscuit Conditionals. I earned my Ph.D. in semantics-pragmatics from Konstanz University with a dissertation titled "Bias Conditions in Polar Questions and Answers: A study of Bias Effects in Farsi".

My visit to UMass Amherst in the fall semester of 2023 enriched my academic experience. Currently, I am a postdoc at Bielefeld University, collaborating on the SFB 1646 project INF.
